01 / The Eduba frame

A working view of a nonprofit with a shared beneficiary pool.

About 60 percent of what happens every day is traditional database and program operations work. About 30 percent is rule-based logic. Around 10 percent is a real AI problem. For the Foundation, the first wedge sits in the 10 percent layer: a two-session AI-literacy and orchestration module for VEP cohorts, calibrated to veteran founders.

The delivery muscle comes from the Correlation One engagements that trained more than 1,500 enterprise learners since May 2025 at Pacific Life and Colgate-Palmolive, with a 95 percent 30-day adoption rate. The cultural alignment comes from a veteran-owned consulting firm founded by a Marine.

The first cohort can be pro-bono. Subsequent cohorts can be underwritten by a named PenFed corporate sponsor or licensed back to the Foundation. The module sits inside the existing VEP curriculum without rewriting what already works.
